Roofing Glossary

Class A Fire Rating

A Class A fire rating is the highest fire-resistance classification a roof assembly can earn — the standard that matters most in wildfire country.

A Class A fire rating is the top classification in the standard fire tests for roof coverings (Class A, B, then C). A Class A assembly resists severe fire exposure: burning brands the size of your hand landing on the surface, flame spreading across the roof, and fire burning through to the decking below. The key word is assembly — the rating belongs to the covering plus the underlayment and layers beneath it, installed as tested, not to a shingle in isolation.

Why it matters in Douglas County

Wildfire is no longer a theoretical risk here — the 2020 Archie Creek Fire burned through the North Umpqua corridor near Glide, and most roof losses in wildfires start with wind-blown embers, not the flame front. Oregon's R327 wildfire hazard code favors Class A roof assemblies, and Douglas County prohibits wood shakes in the Wildfire Hazard Overlay District. The practical good news: the common local choices — asphalt fiberglass composition shingles like quality architectural lines, metal assemblies including standing seam, and tile — all can achieve Class A. Our wildfire-resistant roofing guide walks through the full picture, including the ember-resistant vent and edge details that finish the job.

When it's missing, what it costs you

An unrated or wood-shake roof in a hazard zone can cost you twice: first in real ember vulnerability, and second at insurance renewal, where carriers increasingly discount Class A roofs — or decline homes without them. If you're reroofing anyway, getting Class A costs little or nothing extra with modern materials; compare options on our metal roofing page.

Is my current roof Class A?

Most composition and metal roofs installed to spec in the last two decades are. Wood shake almost never is. If you're unsure, your reroof paperwork or a quick inspection can confirm it — worth knowing before your next insurance conversation.
