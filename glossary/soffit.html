Roofing Glossary

Soffit

A soffit is the finished underside of your roof's overhang — usually vented or perforated so fresh air can flow up into the attic.

Stand under your roof's overhang and look straight up: the surface you see is the soffit. It closes the gap between the wall and the fascia, keeps birds, bats, and wasps out of the attic, and — on most homes — does a quiet, critical job: its vents are the intake half of the attic ventilation system. Cool outside air enters at the soffits, warms, rises, and exits at a ridge vent, keeping the attic dry.

Why it matters in Douglas County

Our winters are the test. From October to May, everyday living — showers, cooking, breathing — pumps moisture into indoor air, and some of it migrates into the attic. With good soffit-to-ridge airflow, it vents harmlessly. With soffits blocked (insulation stuffed into the eave bays and painted-shut vents are the classic culprits around older Roseburg homes), that moisture condenses on the cold underside of the roof deck. The result looks exactly like a roof leak: damp insulation, water stains, even mold — on a roof with nothing wrong up top. Our winter roofing guide covers this in depth.

When it fails, what it costs you

Beyond blocked vents, soffit panels rot when overflowing gutters soak the fascia and the water tracks inward — and open soffits are an invitation to nesting pests. Repairs are usually straightforward carpentry, often bundled with siding and trim work.

Why is there moisture in my attic if the roof isn't leaking?

Nine times out of ten in our climate: condensation from poor ventilation, not a leak. Check that soffit vents are open and unblocked by insulation, and that the air has somewhere to exit at the ridge.
