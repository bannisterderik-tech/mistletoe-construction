Costs & Decisions

How much does a roof replacement cost in Roseburg?

Most asphalt replacements in the Roseburg/Douglas County area land roughly between $10,000 and $25,000 depending on size, pitch, complexity, and deck condition; metal runs 2–3x that upfront. Full breakdown with the seven factors that move price: the cost guide. Your exact number is a free written estimate away.

Should I repair or replace my roof?

Repair when damage is localized and the roof has life left; replace when problems are widespread or the roof is past ~20 years. We'll give you an honest call either way — repairs are a service we're glad to do.

Is a metal roof worth it in the Pacific Northwest?

Often yes: 40–70 year life, sheds moss, Class A fire rating — at 2–3x asphalt's upfront cost. On cost-per-year, metal frequently wins. Honest comparison table in metal vs asphalt.

How long does an asphalt roof last here?

About 20–25 years for architectural shingles — our wet-winter/dry-summer cycle and moss pressure age asphalt faster than drier climates. Maintenance and ventilation push you to the long end.

Rules, Permits & Trust

Do I need a permit to reroof in Douglas County?

Generally yes for work over 100 sq ft — and the county doesn't allow shingle-over-shingle overlays, so a full tear-off is required. We pull permits on every job. Details: the permit guide.

How do I check a contractor's license in Oregon?

Two minutes at the Oregon CCB license search: active status, bond, insurance, complaint history. We're CCB #255729 — here's the step-by-step; look us up first.

Will insurance pay for storm damage to my roof?

For sudden wind/storm damage, often yes minus your deductible — insurance covers events, not wear. Document, mitigate (tarp), then compare your contractor's estimate against the adjuster's. Full playbook: insurance claims guide.

What is a Class A fire-rated roof, and does it help with insurance?

The top fire-resistance rating for roof assemblies — fiberglass asphalt, metal, and tile can qualify. In Douglas County's wildfire environment some insurers discount for it, and it can matter for keeping coverage. Everything you need: wildfire roofing guide.

Moss, Maintenance & Seasons

What are zinc strips — do they stop moss?

Metal strips at the ridge that release growth-inhibiting ions in rain, protecting ~10–15 ft down-slope. Great prevention on a clean roof; useless against an established mat. Full science: the moss guide.

How often should my roof be inspected here?

Annually, in late summer/early fall before the rains — plus after major storms. Our Home Care Membership does this automatically with photo reports.

Can you replace a roof in the winter?

Yes, in the right weather window — Oregon winters have dry stretches, and pro crews dry-in the deck same-day. Sometimes winter scheduling is actually easier. When to do it and when to wait: winter roofing guide.

Why is my ceiling stained when the roof looks fine?

Because most leaks come from flashing, nail pops, or pipe boots — and water travels along rafters before it drips. The stain's location rarely matches the entry point. That's what an inspection with photos is for.

Do gutter guards actually work?

Good ones cut cleaning dramatically, but under fir and oak canopy nothing eliminates maintenance entirely — fine debris still needs an occasional brush-off. Honest details: gutter guards.
