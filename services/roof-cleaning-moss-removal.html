Why It Matters

Moss isn't cosmetic. It's a slow leak in progress.

  • Lifts shingle edges — moss roots wedge under shingles, opening paths for wind-driven rain
  • Holds water against the deck — a moss mat stays wet for days after rain, soaking what's beneath it
  • Accelerates granule loss — the wet-dry cycle under moss ages asphalt faster than anywhere else on the roof
  • Blocks drainage — moss and debris dam valleys and feed clogged gutters
  • Shortens roof life — an unmanaged mossy roof rarely reaches its rated 20–25 years here

Want the full picture — why moss loves western Oregon, how fast it spreads, and what actually stops it? Read our complete roof moss prevention guide.

Our Method

Questions

Moss removal FAQs

Why shouldn't I pressure wash a mossy roof?

Pressure washing strips the protective granules that shield asphalt shingles from UV and rain — it can take years off a roof's life in one afternoon, force water under shingle edges, and void manufacturer warranties. We remove moss by hand with soft brushes and treat what remains, so the moss dies without the shingles paying for it.

How much does moss removal cost around here?

Typical jobs in the Roseburg area run about $250–$600 depending on roof size, pitch, and how established the moss is; heavy multi-year growth or steep rooflines can cost more. We give a written price after seeing the roof — and prevention through the Home Care Membership costs far less than repeated removals.

What's the best season for moss removal?

Late spring through early fall is ideal: the roof is dry, the moss is weakened by our bone-dry summers, and treatments have time to work before October rains restart the growing season. But if moss is already lifting shingles, don't wait — removing it before winter beats letting it hold water against your roof all season. Our moss guide has a season-by-season plan.

Do zinc strips actually work?

Yes, with realistic expectations. A zinc strip near the ridge releases trace zinc every time it rains, inhibiting new growth in the wash zone below — well suited to our 130+ rain days. They prevent regrowth; they don't kill established moss, and heavily shaded areas still need occasional attention. If moss has already done damage, we'll tell you whether a repair or a replacement is the honest next step.
