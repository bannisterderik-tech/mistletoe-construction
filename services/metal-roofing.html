Roofing · Douglas County, OR

Metal roofing: the last roof most homes ever need.

A metal roof sheds our winter rain, refuses to grow the moss that eats asphalt roofs alive, carries a Class A fire rating, and lasts 40–70 years. It costs more upfront — we'll be straight about that — but per year of roof life, it's often the cheapest roof you can buy in the Umpqua Valley.

Why Metal Here

Built for the exact ways Oregon kills roofs.

  • 40–70 year lifespan — two to three times what asphalt manages in our wet-dry climate
  • Sheds moss and debris — the smooth, fast-draining surface gives the region's signature roof problem nowhere to root
  • Handles heavy rain and snow — 30+ inches of annual rain slides off; so does the occasional valley snow load
  • Class A fire rating — the top rating, and increasingly what insurers want to see after the 2020 Archie Creek Fire
  • Wind performance — mechanically seamed or properly fastened panels shrug off the winter storms that peel shingles

Deciding between materials? Our metal vs. asphalt guide for Oregon lays out the trade-offs side by side.

Two Systems

Standing seam or through-fastened — we install both.

Metal roofing isn't one product. The right system depends on your roof, your budget, and how long you plan to stay.

01

Standing seam

Concealed fasteners, panels that lock together and float with temperature swings. The premium option: cleanest look, longest life, no exposed screws to ever re-check.

02

Through-fastened

Exposed-fastener panels screwed to the deck. More affordable, still decades of service — the honest trade-off is gasketed screws that want inspection every decade or so.

03

Fire-smart assemblies

We build Class A assemblies with the right underlayment and ember-resistant details — see our wildfire-resistant roofing guide for why that matters in Douglas County.

04

Full tear-off, done right

Douglas County doesn't allow roofing over old shingles, so every metal roof starts with a clean deck, inspected and repaired — the foundation a 50-year roof deserves.

Straight Talk

Yes, it costs more upfront. Here's the honest math.

Expect roughly 2–3x the price of an asphalt roof — most Douglas County asphalt replacements land roughly $10,000–$25,000, and metal runs well above that. But spread over 40–70 years instead of 20–25, metal is frequently cheaper per year of roof life, and it skips the moss removal cycle asphalt needs here. If asphalt is the better fit for your budget or timeline, we'll say so — shingle replacement is most of what we do.

Questions

Metal roofing FAQs

How much does metal cost compared to asphalt?

Typically about 2–3 times the upfront cost of asphalt. But metal lasts 40–70 years versus roughly 20–25 for asphalt here, so the cost per year of roof life is often lower. We put both numbers in a free written estimate so you can compare honestly — the full comparison guide walks through the math.

Are metal roofs loud in the rain?

Not the way people fear. The drumming sound comes from metal over open framing — a barn or carport. On a home, metal goes over solid decking and underlayment, often with attic insulation below, which dampens rain noise to roughly asphalt levels. Most homeowners can't tell the difference from inside.

Can metal go over my existing shingles?

Plan on a full tear-off — Douglas County doesn't allow new roofing over old shingles. That's good practice anyway: it lets us inspect and repair the deck and flash everything correctly, which is what a 40–70 year roof deserves underneath it. Details in our county permit guide; we handle the permit for you.

How long does a metal roof really last here?

40–70 years properly installed, depending on the panel system and finish — versus roughly 20–25 for asphalt in our wet-dry climate. Metal also sheds the moss and fir debris that shorten asphalt roofs' lives here, and a Home Care Membership inspection keeps fasteners and flashings ahead of any surprises.
