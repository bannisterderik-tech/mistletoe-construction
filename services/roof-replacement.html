Signs It's Time

Is your roof telling you something?

  • 20+ years old — asphalt roofs in western Oregon age faster than the national average thanks to our wet-dry cycle
  • Granules in the gutters — your shingles' armor washing away
  • Curling, cracking, or bald shingles — brittle from summer UV, soaked all winter
  • More than one leak — chasing leaks on a failing roof costs more than it saves
  • Moss you can't stay ahead of — established moss lifts shingle edges and holds water against the deck
  • Sagging ridge or deck lines — possible structural moisture damage underneath

Not sure? A free inspection settles it — and if a repair is the honest answer, that's what we'll tell you.

Questions

Roof replacement FAQs

How long does a roof replacement take?

Most single-family homes take 1–3 days once materials arrive. Complex rooflines, steep pitches, or hidden deck repairs can add a day. We schedule around weather windows — and yes, we can replace roofs in an Oregon winter when conditions allow.

Do I need a permit in Douglas County?

Generally yes for full replacements — and Douglas County doesn't allow new shingles over old ones, so a proper tear-off is required. We handle the permits for you. Our permit guide covers the details.

Repair or replace — how do I know?

One damaged area on an otherwise healthy roof? Repair it. Widespread granule loss, multiple leaks, or 20+ years of age? Replacement usually saves money within a few years. We'll give you an honest answer either way — repairs are a service we're happy to provide.

What shingles do you recommend for this climate?

Architectural shingles with algae-resistant granules are the sweet spot for the Umpqua Valley. As an Owens Corning contractor we typically install the Duration line, and we'll walk you through Class A fire-rated options and metal roofing too.
