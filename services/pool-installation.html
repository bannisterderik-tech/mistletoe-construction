Pool Installation · Douglas County, OR

Pool installation, managed like a construction project.

Umpqua Valley summers run bone-dry with July afternoons around 86°F — a pool here earns its keep. We handle the part most pool brochures skip: site evaluation, excavation, drainage, permits, and coordinating every trade on one schedule, as your licensed local general contractor.

The Honest Version

A pool is a site-work project first.

  • Site evaluation — access for equipment, slope, soil, and where winter water wants to go; the answers shape everything else
  • Excavation & grading — dug, compacted, and drained right, so the surround doesn't settle or pond in the wet season
  • Trade coordination — pool structure, plumbing, and electrical sequenced by one general contractor instead of you refereeing subcontractors
  • Permits & inspections — building, electrical, plumbing, and safety-barrier requirements confirmed with Douglas County and handled for you
  • The finished yard — decking, safety fencing, and landscaping that make it a backyard, not a construction site with water in it

Our roofing roots show up here in a useful way: we're obsessive about where water goes. A pool surround that drains properly through an Oregon winter is the difference between a 30-year pool and a maintenance headache. It's the same discipline we bring to a roof replacement — get the water management right and everything else lasts longer. Want a deck tied into the pool area? Same crew, one plan.

Timing Truth

Want to swim this summer? Plan this winter.

Design, permits, and spring excavation take months, not weeks. Pools planned in December are swimmable in July; pools planned in June are swimmable next year. The free consultation is the low-commitment first step — and winter is when our schedule has the most room.

Questions

Pool installation FAQs

What's involved, start to finish?

Site evaluation and design, permits, excavation and grading, the pool structure, plumbing and electrical by the appropriately licensed trades, backfill and decking, then fencing and final inspection — all coordinated by us on one schedule. Plan in months, not weeks; anyone promising a quick, easy pool is skipping steps you'll pay for later.

When should I start planning?

Winter, if you want to swim this summer. Design, permits, and scheduling take time, and excavation goes best once the ground firms up in late spring — so a pool planned in December is realistically swimmable by the 86-degree July afternoons. Start in June and you're probably swimming next year.

Do you handle excavation and site work?

Yes — that's where a construction company earns its keep on a pool. We evaluate access and slope, manage excavation and spoils, get compaction and drainage right so the surround doesn't settle or pond, and grade so winter runoff moves away from the pool and the house alike.

Do pools need permits here?

Yes — typically building, electrical, and plumbing permits plus safety-barrier requirements before final approval, depending on pool type and property. We confirm the exact requirements with Douglas County, pull the permits, and schedule inspections as part of the job. And once it's built, our Home Care Membership keeps the rest of the property looked after too.
