What We Fix

The repairs we see most in the Umpqua Valley.

  • Active leaks — traced from the attic and the rooftop, not guessed at from the driveway
  • Storm and wind damage — lifted, creased, or missing shingles after a winter blow
  • Flashing failures — chimneys, sidewalls, skylights, and vents are where most Douglas County leaks actually start
  • Valley and penetration wear — the spots that handle the most water on 130+ rain days a year
  • Moss-related damage — lifted shingle edges and soft spots where moss held water against the deck
  • Small deck repairs — soft or rotted sheathing replaced before it spreads

If moss is the root cause, we'll say so — our moss removal service often costs less than the repairs it prevents.

Straight Talk

Repair or replace? We'll tell you the truth.

One damaged spot on a healthy roof is a repair — full stop. But if your roof is past 20 years old with widespread granule loss and multiple leaks, patching it is spending good money on a failing system. We do both jobs, so we have no reason to steer you wrong.

Questions

Roof repair FAQs

How much does a typical roof repair cost?

Most Douglas County repairs land in the low hundreds to low thousands of dollars, depending on access, pitch, and how far water has traveled. A few missing shingles is a small job; rebuilt flashing or deck repair costs more. We give a written price before work starts, and Home Care members get 10% off every repair.

How fast can you get to a leaking roof?

For active leaks we prioritize same-day or next-day visits whenever weather and daylight allow, and members get priority scheduling plus included emergency tarping. Call or text (541) 670-5005 — a quick photo from your phone helps us show up with the right materials.

Will a repair match my existing shingles?

We match manufacturer, profile, and color as closely as possible, but a patch on a roof that's weathered for years will rarely be invisible on day one — new shingles weather toward the old ones over a season or two. We'll show you the closest match first and tell you honestly if it will stand out.

Can you find a leak that only shows in heavy rain?

Yes — wind-driven winter rain pushes water past flashing into paths light rain never finds. We trace stains from the attic side, inspect flashing, valleys, and penetrations up top, and water-test suspect areas when needed. You get photos of what we found, not a guess. Winter leak season tips are in our winter roofing guide.
