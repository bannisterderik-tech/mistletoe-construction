Questions

General contracting FAQs

What kinds of projects do you handle?

Remodels, additions, structural and weather-related repairs, decks, fences, flooring, and the whole-house punch lists that never seem to shrink. If a project touches more than one trade, a general contractor coordinates all of it under one license, one schedule, and one point of contact.

Do you handle permits for remodels and additions?

Yes. We determine what needs a permit, prepare the application, and coordinate inspections with the Douglas County building department. Additions, structural changes, and most significant remodels require permits; small cosmetic work usually doesn't. You shouldn't have to learn the permit code to fix up your own house.

How do estimates work?

Free and in writing. We walk the project with you, then deliver a written scope with labor, materials, and permits broken out — no mystery line items. Larger remodels may take a few days to price accurately because we'd rather quote it right than quote it fast.

Are you licensed for general work, not just roofing?

Yes — Oregon CCB #255729 covers our general contracting work as well as roofing, with the bond and insurance the state requires. You can verify our standing yourself at search.ccb.state.or.us, and our CCB verification guide shows you how. Check any contractor you're considering — including us.
