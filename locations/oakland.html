Local Knowledge

Old-town roofs need more than new shingles.

Oakland grew up along Calapooya Creek in the 1800s, and its brick downtown — rebuilt in the 1890s and preserved as a historic district — sets the tone for the whole town. Many Oakland homes have been standing for a century or more, and their roofs tell that story: original decking under several generations of shingles, brick chimneys with flashing that's been redone half a dozen ways, and steep pitches framed long before modern code.

Working on roofs like these is different. Tear-off has to be an inspection, not a demolition. Douglas County's full tear-off requirement (no overlays) actually serves Oakland well — it forces the decking into daylight where problems can be found and fixed properly. Here's how county permits work.

  • Century-old decking — assessed board by board during tear-off, repaired where needed, documented with photos
  • Brick chimney flashing — the most common leak source on older Oakland homes; done right with step and counter flashing
  • Character-appropriate materials — shingle profiles and colors chosen to suit an older home, not fight it
  • Moss on shaded planes — mature trees and north slopes keep moss pressure high all winter

Questions

Oakland roofing FAQs

Can you reroof an older home without ruining its character?

Yes — that's the point of hiring carefully. Older Oakland homes have steep pitches, original decking, and trim details a rushed crew can wreck. We assess decking honestly during tear-off, repair rather than shortcut, and choose profiles and colors that suit the home. Everything documented with photos.

What roof problems are most common on Oakland's older houses?

Aged decking under many-times-replaced shingles, failing flashing around brick chimneys, sagging gutters, and moss on shaded north planes. Douglas County's full tear-off rule is exactly what a century-old roof deck needs — inspection and repair before anything new goes on.
