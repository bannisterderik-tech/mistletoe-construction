Questions

Landscaping in Myrtle Creek — FAQs

Can landscaping really protect my foundation in Myrtle Creek?

Yes — grading, drainage paths, and smart planting move roof and surface water away from the house instead of against it. Around Myrtle Creek, where winter delivers most of 30+ inches of rain, drainage is the least glamorous, highest-value landscaping decision.

What is fire-wise landscaping for Myrtle Creek properties?

Keep the first five feet around the house lean, clean, and green: no bark mulch against siding, no resinous shrubs under eaves, limbs up off the roof. In Douglas County's fire environment it protects your home and increasingly matters to insurers.

When should landscaping work happen in Myrtle Creek?

Planting favors fall and spring; drainage and hardscape work is best in the dry season when soil cooperates. We plan Myrtle Creek projects around that calendar so nothing gets installed into a mud pit or a drought.
