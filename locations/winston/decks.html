Local Knowledge

Why Winston homes call us for deck building.

Winston sits in Douglas County's banana belt — a little warmer, a little sunnier, which means summer UV works shingles harder here while winter still delivers the county's trademark rain. From the Lookingglass Valley to the neighborhoods around Wildlife Safari, we keep Winston roofs balanced against both.

A deck here has to survive eight wet months to earn four glorious ones. That means ground contact done right, flashing at the ledger (the #1 deck failure point), and materials chosen for moisture — cedar for warmth and workability, composite for set-and-forget.

  • Banana-belt sun: more UV aging on south-facing planes
  • Growing residential areas alongside mid-century core homes
  • Lookingglass Valley wind exposure on open lots

Questions

Deck Building in Winston — FAQs

Cedar or composite for a deck in Winston?

Cedar costs less upfront and feels like Oregon, but wants cleaning and sealing on a schedule. Composite runs more but shrugs off Winston's wet winters with a rinse. We build both and will give you the honest total-cost picture for your use.

Do I need a permit to build a deck in Winston?

Depends on size and height — larger and taller decks around Winston typically permit through Douglas County, and attached decks have structural requirements either way. We know the thresholds and handle the paperwork when it's required.

When is the best time to build a deck in Winston?

We build year-round in weather windows, but late spring through early fall is smoothest. Want it ready for summer? Start the conversation in winter — design, permits, and materials take lead time, and the calendar fills fast.
