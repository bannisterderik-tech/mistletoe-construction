Local Knowledge

We know Riddle roofs because we live under them.

Riddle is a small timber-and-mining town in the Cow Creek valley — the kind of place where the mill whistle and Nickel Mountain are part of local memory, and where most folks know each other by name. That's our story too: Mistletoe Construction is family-owned and headquartered right here in town.

Riddle's housing runs to practical: single-story ranch homes, older farmhouses, manufactured homes, and plenty of shops and outbuildings. The valley gets the same wet-October-through-May pattern as the rest of Douglas County, and the fir-lined hillsides keep shaded roof planes damp — which is why moss prevention is half the battle here.

  • Being in town means being first — storm damage, wind-lifted shingles, or an active leak, we can often be there the same hour
  • Older valley homes — aging shingles and flashing that deserve a straight assessment, not a scare-tactic sales pitch
  • Shops, barns & manufactured homes — we roof the whole property, not just the house
  • Permits handled — Douglas County requires a permit for most reroofs and full tear-off (no overlays); here's how that works
